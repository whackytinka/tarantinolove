Jackie Brown

Jackie Brown is a neo-noir crime film directed by Quentin Tarantino, based on the novel "Rum Punch" by Elmore Leonard. The film follows Jackie Brown, a flight attendant who moonlights as a drug smuggler for a crime boss. Jackie becomes caught in a dangerous game of cat and mouse between the crime boss, a detective, and a young criminal. As she navigates the complexities of her double life, Jackie must make difficult choices that will have far-reaching consequences. Tarantino's signature style, featuring witty dialogue, nonlinear storytelling, and a diverse cast of characters, is on full display in this gritty and engaging crime drama.
